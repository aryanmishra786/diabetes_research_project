# 🩺 Predictive Modeling for Diabetes Mellitus

> Award-Winning Research Project | WEBIST 2025 Best Poster Honorable Mention

A machine learning research project that investigates the impact of oversampling techniques on diabetes prediction using medical history data. The study evaluates whether balancing imbalanced healthcare datasets improves predictive performance without introducing overfitting.

## 📖 Research Overview

Diabetes prediction datasets often suffer from class imbalance, where diabetic cases are significantly underrepresented. This research explores whether oversampling techniques can improve model performance while maintaining strong generalization on unseen patient data.

The study evaluates:

- SMOTE
- SVM-SMOTE
- SMOTEENN

across multiple ensemble machine learning algorithms:

- Random Forest
- Extra Trees
- Gradient Boosting

using extensive cross-validation and performance analysis.

## 📈 Key Findings

### ✅ Oversampling Improves Training Performance

SMOTE and SVM-SMOTE consistently improved training accuracy and F1 scores.

### ⚠️ Risk of Overfitting

Aggressive oversampling methods such as SMOTEENN often produced exceptionally high training scores but weaker generalization on unseen data.

### 🏆 Best Overall Model

Gradient Boosting combined with SVM-SMOTE demonstrated the strongest balance between prediction accuracy and generalization performance.
